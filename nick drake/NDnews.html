    What is a Pink Moon?

    Shameless bragging
    "An amazing amount of information on one of music's most revered songwriters."
    "Because he lived quietly, translated his soul's secrets into soaring songs and received little acclaim in his brief lifetime, British songwriter Nick Drake has won mythical status. One would assume that a testament erected to a man with such a short career (1969-1974) would be packed with editorial lauding instead of hard content. One would be wrong. This assemblage compiles what surely must be the most complete archive of information available on Drake. Besides garnering material from relatives and friends, our Webmaster offers audio of bootleg recordings, scores of guitar tabs and site-specific essays on the artist's life, work and sexual preference. An essential, educational and enjoyable effort."

    (Snap! "Best of the Web"-award for The Nick Drake Files)

    A Nick Drake Film!
    A TV film on Nick is being made in England. This is an announcement from Royston Weekly, November 9, 1995. The headline is: "Got any memories of Nick?" and the piece is written by David Crozier.

    "SINGER songwriter Nick drake, who spent two years in Cambridge studying English in the late 1960s, is the subject of a TV film being made by a Mildenhall company.
        And the company, Sound Techniques Ltd, would like to hear from anyone who knew Nick, who died in 1974 at the age of 26, whilst he was in the city. They hope to make the film, and hopefully an accompanying book, as definitive as possible.
        Nick studied at Fitzwilliam College for two years, dropping out when his first album was released. Understandably many of the songs were written while he lived in Cambridge.
        Although he died over 20 years ago, Nick Drake's music is still admired. Among his fans are current favourites REM, Paul Weller, Oasis and the Boo Radleys. If you knew Nick and would like to contribute to the film, you can write to the film's producer, David Barber, at Sound Techniques Ltd in Hampstead Avenue, Mildenhall, Suffolk, IP28 7AS.
    Alternatively you can ring them on (01638) 713631".

    David Barber in Swedish paper Dagens Nyheter:

    "We've come about half way through. We've interviewed old friends, teachers, musicians. The parents are dead, but I made an interview with his mother four years ago and we've also found some amateur interviews with his parents made 15 years ago. Unfortunately there are no moving images of Nick, but we've got access to all the family photo albums."

    Chance of finding the BBC tapes?
    As all of you know Nick recorded four songs live at BBC's John Peel sessions in 1969. This by now legendary session has so far failed to surface in any recorded form. David Barber at BBC believes the tapes were destroyed after the broadcast, but in an article in Dagens Nyheter, John Peel himself gives us some hope by claiming that the tape may well still exist in the vaults. Perhaps some day... If you've done your homework properly you already know that the songs Nick played at BBC August 5, 1969 were Time Of No Reply, 'Cello Song, River Man and Three Hours.
