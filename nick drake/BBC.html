November, 2000

 The series Rock Shrines is made by BBC Television Documentaries and focuses on significant names in music of the last forty years. Our films are moving and uplifting tributes to various rock and music stars such as Dusty Springfield, Jimi Hendrix, and Jim Morrison, seen through the eyes of their fans and friends. The films are made up of archive footage (of which there is virtually none of Nick!) and music intercut with filming in the vicinity of the stars' shrine or memorial on the day of the anniversary of their passing away. Each film includes interviews with those who have come to pay tribute to these truly memorable musicians, including fans, friends and colleagues.

We have already made films about legendary musicians such as John Lennon (set at Strawberry Fields in New York), Kurt Cobain, Buddy Holly, Jeff Buckley and Jimi Hendrix amongst others. The series will be transmitted on the new BBC digital channel BBC Choice and is likely to be shown on BBC2 in the New Year. The programmes will each be approximately fifteen minutes long.

We are planning to make a film about Nick Drake as part of the series of tributes. The overall aim would be to make a really moving documentary which would clearly show his legacy on music and fans today. We have absolute faith in the fact that we could make a really beautiful film which would give a sense of what made Nick so important to his many fans.

We intend to film on the 25th November at the cemetery in Tanworth in Arden. I have spoken to the vicar at St Mary Magdalene who says that they have fans visiting throughout the year, leaving little gifts so we feel that this is a place where fans can congregate and pay their respects to Nick. We would very much like to talk to any fans who might be going along on the 25th and would also like to encourage people to come along even if they've never been before.

If you're interested or have got any questions: send an e-mail to Kate Patten at the BBC.
