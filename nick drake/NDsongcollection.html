The Nick Drake Song Collection
- reviewed by Chris Healey




Next year will mark twenty-five years since the death of Nick Drake, and the fact that it's taken until now for a songbook to be published says something about the longevity and complexity of his music. THE NICK DRAKE SONG COLLECTION from WISE PUBLICATIONS (a division of MUSIC SALES), is a large, attractively produced book which gives tunings, guitar chord diagrams (not tabs) and strangely, two-stave piano arrangements of 36 songs. All the tracks from the three main albums are here, plus the four songs from the Feb 74 session, and the song TIME OF NO REPLY. Also included is a beautifully done lyrics section, accompanied by both new and well-known photos of Nick and his guitars, and there is a nice foreword by Robert Kirby.

Before passing comment, I should perhaps declare an incidental interest. Having spent years working out the songs for guitar myself, when I heard there was going to be a book I called Music Sales and offered my assistance; they thought about it for a few weeks before deciding that they were confident they'd got the right tunings and transcriptions. I hope it goes without saying that that wouldn't alter my view of it.

So what's it like, technically? On the whole, I feel it's pretty good. It's nice to be able to look at a tuning and a couple of chord diagrams, and be instantly playing something like a Nick Drake song. Certainly faster and less off-putting than tabs. Of course any but the simplest tunes will lack little details, but that's OK - you wouldn't want a page cluttered with chord diagrams for every possible note played. Most of the transcriptions match the tabs I've posted here, which is reassuring.

But it's not all roses. It's my humble opinion, after careful analysis, that eleven of the transcriptions have got the tuning wrong, with effects varying from minor to serious. There are a few others which I feel have irritating faults. And of course, it would have been nice to see more from the TIME OF NO REPLY album. But nonetheless I'd definitely recommend the book (if you can afford the steep asking price) - it's a lovely piece of work which will give tremendous pleasure to any Nick Drake fan who likes to twang a guitar. I just feel that if you're striving for detail and precision (and can cope with tabs) you'll usually benefit from consulting this site. I'd be interested to know what other guitarists think.

Of course there is not and probably never will be a definitive work that we can rely on absolutely. The best we can do is a process of gradual improvement, to which both this book and this site contribute. Maybe in another twenty-five years...

(The book's ISBN is 0-7119-4464-4, and order no. AM92388. Price about £25. My copy says "Not authorised for sale in the USA or Canada", but Music Sales' mail order people in the UK [+44 171 434 0066] told me they'd mail copies anywhere.)
