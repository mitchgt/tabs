NOTES: I used a notation very common in jazz where 9 always means that
also the 7 is included. By the way, it is recommended to omit the 3
in those chords. (As often in jazz-style pieces, 3 and 5 are sometimes
omitted for sake of greater clarity. Try not to play more than 4 strings
in one chord!!!) The piano plays sometimes 9- or 11-chords where I wrote
only 7 or 9, and lots of other coloured stuff.

How to play these chords? Until someone finds something better, I'd re-
commend tuning down the 4th and 6th string one full step to get the
very seldom DACGBE tuning. This is not too different from standard
tuning, so you can basically play the barre chords you're used to;
as long as the bass note is on the 5th string. The 4th and 6th string
are only changed to make the bass notes a bit easier. Here is how the
chords should be played: (Improvements welcome, because I'm no experi-
enced jazz guitar player!)
