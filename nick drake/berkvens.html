April, 2000

 Jeroen lets me know that a viewing of the documentary is taking place in London in April. Joe Boyd and people from big television and film companies will attend, as well as people like Robert Kirby, Keith Morris and Brian Wells, who all appear in the film. /Mikael



February, 2000

 The pre announced documentary A SKIN TOO FEW - THE DAYS OF NICK DRAKE is finished. The producers of the film are organizing a series of 'Nick Drake evenings' in a few big cities, starting in Amsterdam. They also aim to screen the film at coming international festivals and there are plans to release and distribute the film in the near future in such a way that everybody sooner or later will be able to have access to it. Watch these pages for further information.



August, 1999

A Skin Too Few - the days of Nick Drake is a 50 minutes documentary, shot on film and due to come out in projection on international film festivals and broadcasts by various TV-networks from the end of this year on.
  People involved are; Gabrielle Drake, Joe Boyd, John Wood, Robert Kirby, Brian Wells, Keith Morris and Paul Weller.
  It's telling the story of Nick's life in almost chapterlike episodes, starting from early childhood in Burma, mingled with atmospheric images of each period.
  But surely the most important factor is the music, accompanied by dramatic images of the landscapes in and around Tanworth, Cambridge and London. Nick's abandoned Far Leys' room, and most of his original belongings (!) in it, stands out in the film during all seasons and times as a silent witness.
  With the original (!) 8-track of BRYTER LAYTER John Wood and Robert Kirby are able to demonstrate, by separating the tracks, how a Nick Drake song is built up.
  The story is of course inevitably sad, but the film tries to go beyond it, also because we'll see for the first time the long hoped for super-8 footage of a very, very young and lively moving Nick and his family!

    - Jeroen Berkvens
