Full Moon Names

Natives of North America marked the passage of time in reference to the phases of the moon, and used names for the full moon to designate the months. The names of the moons varied from tribe to tribe, but the following list includes the most common names :

January: Wolf Moon
February: Snow Moon
(Passamaquoddy: Hunger Moon; Southern tribes: Opening Buds Moon)
March: Maple Sugar Moon
(Also: Worm Moon)
April: Frog Moon
(Also: Pink Moon, Planter's Moon)
May: Flower Moon
(Huron: Budding Moon)
June: Strawberry Moon
July: Blood Moon
(Also: Buck Moon)
August: Moon of the Green Corn
(Also: Sturgeon Moon)
September: Harvest Moon
October: Hunter's Moon
(Cree: Moon of Falling Leaves)
November: Beaver Moon
December: Cold Moon


Source: "Marking the Months," Diane E. Haspel and Barbara Knutson, _Odyssey_, November, 1993, vol. 2, issue 9, p2.]
