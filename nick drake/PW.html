One of the privileges of running a website like this one is the spin-off effect of discovering other artists, those hidden treasures that I perhaps wouldn't have found out about, if it wasn't for the numerous e-mail tips I've received and all the references that I've picked up from articles and mailing list discussions.

Fred Neil, John Martyn, Everything But The Girl, Smog, Love, Ida, Tim Hardin and Mark Eitzel are just a few examples of artists I have discovered by following the crooked roads that one way or another lead from or to Nick Drake.

I can now add Paul Wheeler to that list. Ever since I read Arthur Lubow's essay (that came with the FRUIT TREE box set) more than ten years ago, I have been aware of Paul Wheeler's connection to Nick and the Cambridge circuit, and I knew that it was he who brought Nick and John Martyn together, but I hadn't had the pleasure of hearing any of his music until just a few months ago. RAIN OVER THE ISLAND is Paul's first proper album release, and it's a summary of 30 years of song-writing. It includes songs that Nick heard before he made his first album, and it includes a song that has been covered by John Martyn. This page is an introduction to his works.

Mikael Ledin, January 1999
